3 Protein Overview

The mouse protein Bsep, also known as Bile Salt Export Pump, is encoded by the gene Abcb11. It has several aliases, including Abcb11 and Bsep. This protein is primarily involved in the transport of bile acids from the liver to the bile ducts, playing a crucial role in bile formation and overall liver function.

4 Data Overview

The protein data can be handled either with RINT normalization or using box-cox transformations. Here, I demonstrate the data’s properties and the BC transformation chosen. Often the BC transformation resulted in more clear genome scans, but not all BC transformed data has undergone complete analysis.

4.5 ### TIMBR

TIMBR (Tree-Based Inference of Multiallelism via Bayesian Regression) is a framework for inferring the allelic series at a QTL in multiparental populations. Instead of assuming each founder haplotype is functionally unique, TIMBR tests whether some haplotypes can be grouped together into fewer functional alleles that better explain trait variation. The method uses a prior, which in Bayesian statistics means the assumptions or expectations built into the model before the data are analyzed. In TIMBR, the prior is based on the Chinese Restaurant Process (CRP), which is a flexible way to let the data decide how many functional alleles there are. Under the CRP, haplotypes are more likely to be assigned to an allele group that already has members, but new groups can always form. TIMBR also incorporates phylogenetic information so that closely related haplotypes are more likely to cluster together, which reflects biological intuition. By doing this, TIMBR can distinguish whether a QTL is best explained by a simple two-allele model, or whether multiple functional alleles are truly required. 4.6 ### Mediation

bmediatR (Bayesian mediation analysis for QTL) is designed to test whether molecular traits act as mediators of genetic effects. A mediator is a variable that explains the path between a genetic variant and another trait. For example, if a transcript level sits between a genetic variant and a protein trait, the transcript is considered the mediator; if a protein explains the link between a variant and a downstream phenotype, the protein is the mediator. The model formally compares alternative causal structures: one where the genetic effect is direct, and others where the effect is indirect through a transcript or protein. Another key concept is co-localization (co-local), which refers to cases where two traits share a QTL in the same genomic region. Co-localization alone does not prove mediation, since different causal variants may lie close together, but bmediatR provides a framework to test whether the shared QTL is consistent with a mediation model. This helps clarify whether, for example, a transcript QTL drives a protein QTL, or whether they simply overlap without a causal link.
